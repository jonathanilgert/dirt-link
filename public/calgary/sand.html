Sand types and their uses

  • Mason sand — finely graded, used in mortar mixes for bricks, blocks, and stone work.
  • Concrete sand — coarser, sharp-edged sand used in concrete and as paver base bedding.
  • Fill sand — unscreened or coarsely screened sand used for trench backfill and structural fill.
  • Paver sand / leveling sand — fine sand swept between pavers and used as the bedding layer under them.
  • Play sand — washed, screened sand for sandboxes and play surfaces.
  • Pipe bedding sand — graded sand specified for utility trenches.

How much sand do you need?

  • Paver base bedding (1" deep): 1 cubic yard per 320 sq ft
  • Mortar mix: ~1 cubic yard per 50 sq ft of single-wythe brick wall
  • Sandbox (12" deep): 1 cubic yard per 27 sq ft

Frequently asked questions

What sand do I use under pavers?
Concrete sand (sometimes called "bedding sand") for the base layer; paver sand or polymeric sand for joints.
Is play sand and mason sand the same?
No — play sand is washed and screened to remove silica fines and small particles. Mason sand is graded for mortar and is not safe for play use.

What's the difference between sharp sand and mason sand?
Sharp sand has coarser, angular grains — used in concrete. Mason sand has finer grains — used in mortar.
