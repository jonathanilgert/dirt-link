Types of fill dirt

  • Clean fill — fill with no organic material, debris, asphalt, or contaminants. The default spec for most municipal and engineered work.
  • Structural fill / engineered fill — fill that meets a specific gradation and compaction spec. Required for foundations, retaining walls, and load-bearing applications.
  • Common fill — general-purpose fill for non-structural grading, landscaping, and low-spot filling.
  • Site-stripped dirt — native soil pulled from active Calgary excavation sites. Variable quality. Often free or very low cost.
  • Topsoil-grade fill — higher organic content, lower bearing capacity, used for top layers and landscaping fill.

Most Calgary fill on the market comes from active basement digs, foundation excavations, and grading projects in growth corridors — Cornerstone, Livingston, Glacier Ridge in the NE; Mahogany, Seton, Walden in the SE; Trinity Hills, Greenwich in the NW. Calgary fill dirt pricing

  • Free fill — common for clean fill coming off active job sites where the alternative is trucking time, scale-house waits, and (for commercial loads) Calgary's clean-fill approval process. Often you cover hauling.
  • Paid fill — typical when material is engineered, screened, or in short supply.
  • Avoid the surcharge tier — Calgary clean fill is cheap to dump ($0–$10/tonne), but a load with sod, mixed debris, or contaminated material gets billed at $113/tonne (basic sanitary) or $180/tonne (commercial surcharge). Listing on Dirtlink keeps non-pristine loads out of that tier entirely.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I get free fill dirt in Calgary?
See the Free Fill Dirt page for live listings. Most free fill comes from active Calgary excavation sites where the alternative is paying tipping fees.
What's the difference between fill and topsoil?
Fill is structural — used to build up grade, fill voids, and form a base for foundations or landscaping. Topsoil is the organic-rich top layer used for growing.
Is Calgary fill dirt clean?
Listings on Dirtlink describe the source and any known contaminants. Clean fill should have no organics, asphalt, concrete, or wood debris. Always verify before accepting a load.
How is fill dirt sold?
By the cubic yard or by the load. A standard tandem load is ~14–16 cubic yards.
Do I need a soil report?
For non-structural homeowner fill, no. For engineered fill in structural applications, yes — and the supplier should provide gradation and Proctor data.
