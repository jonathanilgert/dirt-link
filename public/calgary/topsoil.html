Types of topsoil sold in Calgary

  • Screened topsoil: Run through a 1/4" or 1/2" screen to remove rocks, roots, and debris. The default for lawns and gardens.
  • Black topsoil: Higher organic content, darker colour, often blended with peat or compost. Common ask for new sod and garden beds.
  • Pulverized topsoil: Finely broken-down topsoil — easier to spread thin over an existing lawn for top-dressing.
  • Site-stripped topsoil: Native topsoil pulled from a Calgary excavation site. Lower cost, variable quality. Worth checking for rocks and clay content.
  • Loam blend: A topsoil/sand/compost mix tuned for drainage and growth — see the Loam page for details.

Frequently asked questions

How much does topsoil cost in Calgary?
Pricing depends on screening, organic content, and load size. Yard-bought screened topsoil is the premium tier; site-stripped material from active Calgary excavations is the budget tier. Live listings on Dirtlink show current pricing across both.
What's the best topsoil for new sod in Calgary?
A screened topsoil with moderate organic content — too much organic and the soil compacts; too little and the sod struggles to root. Many Calgary suppliers offer a "lawn blend" that's tuned for this.
How much topsoil for a raised garden bed?
Roughly 1 cubic yard fills a 4'x8' bed at 12" deep. Most Dirtlink listings sell minimum 1-yard quantities.
Is screened topsoil worth the extra cost?
For lawns, gardens, and any visible application, yes — unscreened topsoil can have rocks, roots, and clay clumps that fail in finished work. For deep fill applications, unscreened is fine.
Can I get same-day topsoil delivery in Calgary?
Often yes during peak season (May–September). Filter Dirtlink listings by "same-day available."
