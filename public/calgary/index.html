Calgary by the numbers

  • Active developments in Cornerstone, Livingston, Carrington, Mahogany, Auburn Bay, Seton, Walden, Trinity Hills, and Symons Valley produce constant demand and supply imbalances that Dirtlink helps balance.
  • Spring road bans (typically late March through early June) tighten hauling windows — Dirtlink helps you stage moves before and after the ban.
  • Calgary clean-fill tipping is actually cheap ($0–$10/tonne) — but the moment a load includes sod, mixed debris, or anything contaminated, the rate jumps to $113–$180/tonne. Add commercial approval numbers, possible soil analysis, scale-house wait times, and the trucking round-trip, and the real disposal cost climbs fast for any non-pristine load.

Frequently asked questions

How does Dirtlink Calgary work?
List what you have or what you need. Other Calgarians match with you directly. Most matches happen the same day in active markets.
Is it free to list?
Yes. Listing is free for both supply and demand.
Do you handle delivery?
No — Dirtlink is the matching layer. You and the other party arrange hauling. Many listings come with hauling included; many haulers list directly.
What areas do you cover?
The Calgary metro plus surrounding markets — Airdrie, Cochrane, Okotoks, Chestermere, Strathmore, Bragg Creek, High River.
Can I post if I'm not a contractor?
Yes. Homeowners with extra fill or who need topsoil for landscaping post regularly.
