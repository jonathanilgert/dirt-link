Mulch types in Calgary

  • Cedar mulch — fragrant, natural insect-deterrent properties, slow to break down.
  • Hemlock mulch — reddish, popular for ornamental beds.
  • Wood chip mulch — coarser, often available cheap or free from arborists.
  • Bark mulch — chunky pine or fir bark, longer-lasting.
  • Coloured mulch — dyed wood mulch in black, brown, and red.

How much mulch do you need?

At a typical 2–3" depth: 1 cubic yard covers ~80–130 sq ft. A standard 8'x10' bed at 3" depth needs about 1 cubic yard.

Calgary mulch sourcing tip

Some Calgary arborists list wood chip mulch on Dirtlink for free or near-free — they'd otherwise pay to dispose of it. If you're not picky about chip size or species mix, free arborist mulch is the cheapest option in the city.

Frequently asked questions

What's the best mulch for Calgary?
Cedar and bark mulches handle Calgary's freeze-thaw and dry summers well. Wood chip is fine for utility beds and play areas.
How often do I replace mulch?
Top up annually; full replacement every 3–5 years depending on type.
Does mulch attract pests?
Cedar repels some pests. Untreated wood chips can attract insects in very wet conditions. Most Calgary applications are fine.
Is free mulch from arborists safe?
Yes for general landscaping. Avoid for vegetable gardens unless you know the source species.
How much does mulch cost in Calgary?
Cedar and coloured mulches are the premium tier; arborist wood chip is the budget tier. Live listings show current pricing.
