How "wanted" listings work

  1. Post the type of fill you need, the quantity, and your location.
  2. Calgary excavators paying to dump material see your post and match.
  3. You typically pay only for hauling (or arrange your own).
  4. They save the trucking time and (for non-pristine loads) the $113–$180/tonne sanitary rate. You get the fill.

What to include in a wanted listing

  • Quantity needed (cubic yards or loads)
  • Soil spec (clean fill, structural, topsoil)
  • Site access (truck size limits, gate access, gradient)
  • Pickup window (when you can receive)
  • Spreading equipment (whether you have a loader on site)

Typical Calgary use cases

  • Backyard regrading and low-spot filling
  • Building up a pad for a shed, garage, or RV parking
  • Acreage grading in surrounding markets (Cochrane, Okotoks, etc.)
  • Pre-landscaping bulk fill before topsoil

Frequently asked questions

Is it really free?
Often yes. The supply side saves trucking hours and (for any load that isn't pristine clean fill) the $113–$180/tonne basic sanitary or commercial surcharge rates. You typically arrange and pay for hauling only.
How fast do wanted listings get filled?
During peak season (April–October), often within hours.
What's "clean fill"?
Soil with no debris, asphalt, concrete, organics, or contaminants. Suitable for non-structural grading.
Do I need engineering for the fill I receive?
For homeowner grading and landscape fill, no. For structural applications, yes — verify the spec.
Can I take partial loads?
Some listings yes, some no. Specify in your wanted listing whether partials work.
