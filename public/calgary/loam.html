What loam is

Loam is a blended soil — typically topsoil, sand, and compost in tuned proportions. Each Calgary supplier blends their own recipe; the differences matter for specific uses.

Common Calgary loam blends

  • Lawn loam — tuned for sod establishment and turf growth.
  • Garden loam — higher organic content for vegetables and ornamentals.
  • Raised bed mix — heavy compost component for new beds.
  • Premium screened loam — fine-screened for top-dressing.

How much loam do you need?

  • New lawn (4" depth): 1 cubic yard per 80 sq ft
  • Raised bed (12" depth): 1 cubic yard per 27 sq ft

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between loam and topsoil?
Topsoil is generally site-stripped or basic-screened soil. Topsoil is the cheaper option; loam is a blended product with controlled proportions of topsoil, sand, and compost.
Is loam worth the extra cost?
For lawns, raised beds, and any application where growth quality matters — yes. For deep fill, basic topsoil is fine.
Can I make my own loam?
Yes — blending equal parts topsoil, sand, and compost gets you in the ballpark. Pre-blended loam is consistent and saves time.
What's the best loam for Calgary lawns?
A blend with moderate organic content (too much and the soil compacts; too little and turf struggles).
