River rock sizes

  • 3/4" – 1.5" — small ground cover and accent
  • 1.5" – 3" — most common size for general landscaping
  • 3" – 5" — feature stones and dry creek beds
  • 5"+ — accent and erosion control

Calgary uses

  • Ground cover in beds — alternative to mulch, longer-lasting
  • Dry creek beds — drainage feature with naturalistic look
  • Around downspouts — drainage and splash protection
  • Path edging and accent
  • Around hot tubs and water features

How much river rock do you need?

At 2–3" depth: 1 cubic yard covers ~80–130 sq ft.

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between river rock and crushed rock?
River rock is rounded and smooth from natural water tumbling. Crushed rock is angular from mechanical crushing. Crushed compacts; river rock doesn't.
Can I walk on river rock?
Smaller sizes (under 1.5") work for occasional foot traffic. Larger sizes are decorative only.
Does river rock prevent weeds?
Better than no cover, but landscape fabric underneath is essential.
How deep should river rock be?
2–3" minimum for visual coverage; 3–4" for effective weed suppression.
