Dirt Disposal in Calgary — Skip the Scale House, Match with a Local Taker

Calgary clean-fill tipping is actually cheap on paper — $0 to $10 per tonne — but the disposal cost isn't the tipping rate, it's everything around it. Trucking hours, scale-house waits, commercial clean-fill approval numbers, possible soil analysis, and the very real risk that one yard of sod or mixed debris flips your load into the $113–$180/tonne basic sanitary tier. Dirtlink solves the whole problem differently: list your fill on the marketplace, match with a homeowner or contractor who needs it, and skip the scale house entirely.

How dirt disposal through Dirtlink works

  1. Post a listing — soil type, quantity, location, pickup window.
  2. Get matched within hours during peak season.
  3. The taker arranges hauling (or uses a Dirtlink-paired hauler).
  4. You save the trucking hours, the scale-house wait, and (for non-clean loads) the $113–$180/tonne sanitary rate. They get the fill they needed. Win-win.

When this works best

  • Clean fill from basement, foundation, and grading work
  • Surplus topsoil from landscape jobs
  • Site-stripped material from active builds
  • Excess material from utility trenching (must be clean)

When you still need the landfill

  • Contaminated material (asphalt, concrete, organics, hazardous)
  • Material that can't wait for a match
  • Tight pickup windows that don't fit listing turnaround

For everything else, listing on Dirtlink first costs nothing and almost always saves money.

Note that "clean fill" has rules — commercial vehicles need a clean-fill approval number from the City, and a soil analysis may be required. Sod, despite being organic, is not treated as compost or clean fill — it's billed at the basic sanitary rate.

Frequently asked questions

How much can I save with Dirtlink vs. the landfill?
Depends entirely on what's in your load. For pristine clean fill, the tipping rate itself is cheap — most savings come from cutting trucking time and avoiding the City's commercial approval process. For loads with sod, mixed debris, or any contamination, savings are direct: the landfill bills $113–$180/tonne for that material, and Dirtlink can often place it for free.
How fast do listings get matched?
During peak season (April–October), often within hours. Off-season runs slower but most clean-fill listings still match within a few days.
Is there a fee to list fill for disposal?
No. Listing is free.
What if my fill has some debris?
List it as "mixed" or "non-clean fill" and you'll still match with takers — usually for non-structural applications. Be honest about what's in it.
Who hauls the fill?
Most often the taker. Some listings include hauling on the supply side. Dirtlink also lists haulers separately.
