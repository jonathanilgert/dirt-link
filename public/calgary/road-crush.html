What road crush is

Road crush is crushed gravel sized at 20mm and below, including fines (the small particles that lock the rock together when compacted). The fines are what differentiate road crush from clear crush — they're what makes the material compact into a hard, durable surface.

Common Calgary uses

  • Driveways — 4–6" of road crush over a compacted subbase.
  • Parking pads — same spec.
  • Shed and RV pads — 4" minimum, well-compacted.
  • Roadway base — under asphalt or concrete in commercial work.
  • Trench backfill — when a compactable spec is needed.

How much road crush do you need?

  • 4" deep: 1 cubic yard per 80 sq ft
  • 6" deep: 1 cubic yard per 55 sq ft

A typical 20'x40' driveway at 4" depth needs ~10 cubic yards.

Recycled vs. virgin road crush

Recycled crushed concrete (RAP-concrete) is often sold as a road crush alternative in Calgary at a lower price point. It compacts well and is suitable for most non-engineered applications. See the Recycled Concrete page.

Frequently asked questions

What size is road crush in Calgary?
20mm minus is the standard. Smaller specs (12mm, 10mm) are available for finer surface work.
How much road crush for a driveway in Calgary?
A 20'x40' driveway at 4" depth needs about 10 cubic yards.
Should I compact road crush?
Yes — without compaction it stays loose and doesn't perform. Plate compactor for small jobs, roller for larger.
Can I use road crush under pavers?
Yes, road crush is the standard paver base material. Top with a 1" layer of paver/concrete sand before laying pavers.
